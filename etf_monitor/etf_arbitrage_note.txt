# ETF Arbitrage Trap Notes (HK-listed A-share ETFs)

> Audience: an LLM (GPT-5.4 class) that will reason about whether/how to trade
> the discount-mean-reversion strategy implemented around `etf_live_monitor.py`.
> Format: dense, declarative, English+Chinese mixed where Chinese is the
> precise local term. Each item is "FACT: <fact> -> IMPLICATION: <what to do>".
> Last updated 2026-04 by human + Copilot pair.

---

## 0. One-line strategy thesis

When ETF market price (`P`) deviates from the official intraday estimated
NAV (`iNAV`, HKD-converted), buy the cheap side and wait for convergence.

Discount in basis points:
    bps = (P - iNAV) / iNAV * 1e4
- bps <  0  -> discount, P below fair value -> potential BUY
- bps >  0  -> premium,  P above fair value -> potential SELL/avoid

Mean reversion is not free money. The traps below are what kill the trade.

## 2. The structural session-mismatch trap (CRITICAL)

### 2.1 Trading hours (all in Asia/Shanghai = Asia/Hong_Kong = UTC+8)

```
A-share (SSE/SZSE):  09:30-11:30 , 13:00-15:00          (4h)
HK equities (SEHK):  09:30-12:00 , 13:00-16:00          (5.5h)
```

### 2.2 What happens in each window

| HK time         | A-share state   | iNAV behavior                              | Implication                                                |
|-----------------|-----------------|--------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| 09:30-11:30     | open            | live, ticking ~5s                          | normal mean-reversion regime; basis is tradable            |
| 11:30-13:00     | LUNCH (closed)  | FROZEN at 11:30:00 print                   | iNAV is stale. ETF price keeps moving on HK flow + leaks   |
| 13:00-15:00     | open            | live again                                 | normal regime                                              |
| 15:00-16:00     | CLOSED          | FROZEN at 15:00:00 print (or close NAV)    | ETF still trades in HK with no underlying price discovery  |

### 2.3 Why this is a trap, not an opportunity

When A-share is closed, the HK ETF price BECOMES the price-discovery
instrument. Smart money trades the ETF based on news, futures (FTSE A50
futures on SGX run longer), and mainland sentiment, while iNAV stays at the
last A-share print.

Naive interpretation:
  "ETF price rose 50bps above iNAV in the lunch break -> premium -> short!"
Reality:
  "ETF price rose because A50 SGX futures rose 60bps; iNAV will gap up at
  13:00 reopen. The 'premium' was an information leading indicator, not
  mispricing."

Empirically the ETF leads iNAV during A-share closure with R^2 typically
> 0.7 against SGX A50 futures movement. Trading on the stale iNAV is
selecting against yourself.

### 2.4 Defensive rule

NEVER signal on `bps` when A-share is closed unless you have explicitly
modeled the futures-implied iNAV. The monitor should expose:
- `a_share_open` (bool)
- `nav_staleness_seconds` (int) = now_utc - parse(inav_time)

Filter: trade only when `a_share_open == True AND nav_staleness_seconds < 30`.

## 3. iNAV pipeline traps

### 3.1 Update cadence

- ICE publishes iNAV roughly every 5s during A-share hours.
- Our poller defaults to 15s; this UNDERSAMPLES. Lower to 5s if alpha
  decays faster than poll interval.
- If poll fails (network, ICE 5xx), the previous row is gone but the
  monitor writes an error row. Backtests must filter `official_inav_error == ""`.

### 3.2 RMB -> HKD conversion

iNAV_HKD = iNAV_RMB * (RMB/HKD spot)
- The FX rate ICE uses is sampled at iNAV publish time. It is NOT a
  real-time CNH/HKD or USDHKD-derived rate. Mismatch can be 5-15 bps in
  stress.
- Implication: a "discount" of < 15 bps may be entirely FX noise. Set the
  trade trigger threshold > 30 bps to clear FX uncertainty.

### 3.3 iNAV vs end-of-day NAV

- iNAV = intraday estimate from index basket * static creation basket.
- End-of-day NAV = actual fund accounting NAV with fees, cash drag,
  dividend accrual.
- Daily drift between iNAV and EoD NAV of 5-20 bps is normal. Do NOT
  conclude "all discounts mean-revert to zero". They mean-revert to the
  iNAV-vs-NAV bias of the specific ETF, which is usually slightly negative
  (fund slightly underperforms index due to fees).

### 3.4 IB's own `ib_nav_last` (tick types 96-99)

- IB's NAV tick is a separate calculation, sometimes proprietary, often
  staler than ICE iNAV by 30-60s.
- Use ICE iNAV as the ground truth; treat IB NAV as redundant cross-check.
- `latest_nav_ticks()` reads from `ticker.ticks` (a deque ib_insync may
  truncate). Don't rely on it for historical reconstruction; rely on the
  CSV log.

## 4. Execution and microstructure traps

### 4.1 Half-spread cost

For HK ETFs, typical bid-ask spreads (in bps of mid):
- 2800, 2828, 2822, 3188:  3-8 bps   (liquid)
- 3037, 3033:              8-20 bps  (medium)
- 3115, smaller issuers:   20-50 bps (thin)

Round-trip cost = 2 * half-spread + 2 * commission + stamp duty (HK = 0
for ETF) + SFC levy (~0.3 bps) + clearing fee (~0.4 bps).

Total round-trip floor: ~10 bps for liquid names, ~50 bps for thin names.

DO NOT trade discounts smaller than 2x round-trip cost. The signal must
clear cost AND have positive edge after that.

### 4.2 Asymmetric execution side

`bps_ask` = (ask - iNAV)/iNAV*1e4 -> if very negative, you can BUY at ask
                                     and capture the discount on entry.
`bps_bid` = (bid - iNAV)/iNAV*1e4 -> if very positive, you can SELL at bid
                                     and capture the premium on entry.
`bps_mid` is for analysis only; you cannot trade at mid unless you post.

The monitor logs `ask_ib_nav_discount_bps`, `bid_ib_nav_discount_bps`,
`last_ib_nav_discount_bps`. The triggering field for a long entry is
`ask_ib_nav_discount_bps`, NOT mid.

### 4.3 Posting passive vs hitting active

Posting a buy at bid and waiting captures spread but you sit in the queue.
On HK ETFs the queue is shallow; if iNAV moves against you, your order
gets adversely selected.
Hitting the ask costs the spread but is fast.
Plan: model both. Default to hitting for retail-scale capital.

### 4.4 Short-side feasibility

- HK ETFs are shortable IF on the SEHK Designated Securities for Short
  Selling list AND IB has borrow. Many A-share ETFs have very thin borrow.
- Borrow rate can be 5-30% annualized in stress -> destroys the carry of a
  multi-day mean-reversion trade.
- Implication: the long-discount side is more reliably tradable than
  short-premium. Strategy should be biased long-only or use index futures
  (FTSE A50 SGX, HSCEI HKFE) as the short hedge.

### 4.5 No retail creation/redemption

True ETF arbitrage uses creation/redemption with the AP (authorized
participant) at end-of-day NAV against a basket of A-shares. Retail
cannot do this. Therefore retail "arb" is purely directional bet on
mean reversion of `P-iNAV`. Never call it arbitrage internally; call it
`ETF basis trade` or `discount mean reversion`.

## 5. Calendar / holiday traps

- HK holidays != mainland holidays. Examples that matter:
  - HK Easter / Good Friday: HK closed, A-share open. No problem here, we
    just don't trade.
  - Mainland Golden Week / Spring Festival: A-share closed for a week, HK
    open. iNAV is FROZEN for days. Discounts can blow out 100-300 bps and
    NOT mean-revert until A-share reopens. DO NOT trade these windows.
- Half-day HK trading (Christmas Eve, NYE, CNY eve): A-share session-
  mismatch logic must include HK early close.
- Northbound stock-connect closures (e.g. T+1 settlement days, public
  holidays in either market): in-kind creation/redemption for A-share ETFs
  may pause -> APs cannot arb -> structural discount widens. This is a
  REGIME, not noise. Detect via `dataframe groupby(date).std(bps)` spikes.

## 6. Latency reality for retail

- IB Gateway top-of-book tick latency: typically 50-250ms from exchange.
- ICE iNAV latency: 1-5s aggregation + ~100-300ms HTTP.
- Our writer adds <10ms; our 3-thread design adds <50ms scheduling jitter.

So the freshest snapshot the monitor can stamp has internal asynchrony of
~250ms-5s between the IB price half and the iNAV half. The `state_age_ms`
column makes this explicit.

For a retail trader who clicks/sends an order, total reaction is 500ms-2s
on top. HFT competitors are <1ms colo. Therefore:
- Do NOT chase signals that decay in <5s.
- Target signals with half-life >= 30s. Discount mean-reversion at this
  scale is plausible; tick-by-tick basis trading is not.

## 7. Mean-reversion failure modes (when to NOT trade)

1. Regime break: ETF AP backed off (creation/redemption suspended). The
   discount becomes structural and may widen further. Detect via:
   `rolling 5-day median(bps) drifting > 20 bps`.
2. Underlying halt: an A50 constituent (e.g. CATL, Kweichow Moutai) is
   halted. iNAV uses last price and is wrong by however much that name has
   moved on news. Correct iNAV via futures-implied basket is non-trivial.
3. Index event: addition/deletion days, the basket is being rebalanced;
   AP behavior is anomalous around close.
4. Ex-dividend day on A-share constituents: iNAV drops by dividend amount
   instantaneously; ETF price may not reflect immediately if HK is on
   different cash-tracking convention.
5. CNH/CNY divergence: in stress (e.g. RMB devaluation episodes), CNH/CNY
   gap widens; FX-translated iNAV becomes ambiguous.

For each, the protective rule: if rolling 1-hour z-score of `bps` is > 3,
do NOT enter a new position; manage existing position only.

## 8. What `etf_live_monitor.py` records and what each file is for

| File suffix              | Purpose                                              |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| `_ib_ticks.csv`          | Every IB L1 tick. Tick-level reconstruction.         |
| `_ib_depth.csv`          | L2 DOM updates (when --with-depth). Liquidity model. |
| `_official_inav.csv`     | One row per ICE poll. Ground truth iNAV series.      |
| `_sync_snapshot.csv`     | Joined view: iNAV row + latest IB state at poll time |
| `ib_nav_probe.csv`       | One-shot probe at startup; diagnostic only.          |

Backtest entry point: `_sync_snapshot.csv`. Filter:
- `official_inav_error == ""`
- `state_age_ms < 30000` (data fresh enough)
- `a_share_open == True`  (when this column lands; see section 2.4)
- `nav_staleness_seconds < 30`

Then compute features and labels off `bps`, `bps_ask`, `bps_bid`.

## 10. Things this note explicitly does NOT claim

- That mean reversion of HK A-share ETF basis is statistically significant
  net of costs at retail scale. (Open question. Prove it before sizing.)
- That ICE iNAV is unbiased. (Section 3.3 says it isn't.)
- That `etf_live_monitor.py` is sufficient for production trading. It is
  a research data recorder. Trade execution would need additional
  components: order manager, position book, risk limits, kill switch.

## 11. Glossary (for downstream LLM)

- iNAV: intraday estimated net asset value, published periodically.
- AP: authorized participant. Broker-dealer entitled to create/redeem
  shares with the issuer at NAV against the underlying basket.
- Creation basket: list of A-share holdings + cash that = 1 creation unit.
- bps: basis points. 1 bps = 0.01% = 0.0001.
- L1 / top-of-book: best bid + best ask + last trade.
- L2 / DOM (depth of market): N levels per side.
- Stock Connect: cross-border trading link Northbound (HK->Mainland) and
  Southbound (Mainland->HK).
- Frozen NAV / stale NAV: iNAV not updating because underlying market
  closed or feed broken.
- Basis: P_etf - iNAV (in price units) or in bps.
- Half-life: time for a signal to decay to half its initial magnitude.
